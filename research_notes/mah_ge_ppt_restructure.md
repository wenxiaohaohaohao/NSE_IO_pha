## 1. What the current deck does well

- It already has a clear institutional background.
- It already documents MAH and explains why the old bundled regime mattered.

## 2. What the current deck is missing

- It does not yet show actual **state transitions** across firm types.
- It does not yet separate **entry composition** from **incumbent switching**.
- It says "firm differentiation" but not yet "firm boundaries and reorganization."
- It has no explicit GE language on producer profit, contract-manufacturing demand, or welfare.

## 6. One-slide summary you can use immediately

Suggested title:

**MAH changes not only innovation incentives, but also the organization of innovation**

Suggested bullet points:

- Pre-MAH, innovation ownership and production capacity were institutionally bundled.
- MAH allows R&D firms to retain ownership while outsourcing production.
- This changes entry, survival, and transitions across integrated, R&D-specialized, and production-specialized firms.
- The resulting reorganization affects innovation, producer profits, and welfare in equilibrium.

## 7. Slide to add right after the current mechanism section

Suggested title:

**From firm differentiation to firm-state transitions**

Suggested content:

- Types: integrated `A`, R&D-specialized `B`, production-specialized `C`
- Entry: `E -> B/C`
- Switching: `A -> B`, `A -> C`, `B -> A`, `C -> A`
- Exit from all active types
- MAH lowers commercialization cost and switching cost

This is the bridge slide that will make the whole deck feel like a new paper.
